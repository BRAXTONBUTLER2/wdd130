Highest Altitude Jump:

Alan Eustace set a record in 2014 by jumping from a helium balloon at an altitude of 135,890 feet, breaking the sound barrier during his descent.

Most Jumps in 24 Hours:

Jay Stokes completed 640 jumps in a 24-hour period in 2006, setting a world record for the most jumps in a single day.

Oldest Skydiver:

In 2019, Bryson William Verdun Hayes became the oldest skydiver at the age of 101. He made a tandem jump to celebrate his centenary.

Fastest Freefall Speed:

Felix Baumgartner broke the speed of sound during his famous Red Bull Stratos jump in 2012, reaching a maximum freefall speed of 843.6 mph (1,357.6 km/h).

Longest Freefall Time:

In 2016, Luke Aikins completed a jump from 25,000 feet without a parachute and landed safely in a net. He achieved the longest freefall without a parachute.

Largest Formation:

In 2015, 164 jumpers from 7 different countries set a record for the largest formation skydive, creating a formation in the shape of a giant flower.

Wingsuit Records:

Multiple records exist for wingsuit flying, including the farthest distance flown, longest time in a wingsuit, and highest altitude jump wearing a wingsuit.

Jumping Without a Parachute and Surviving:

In 2007, Michael Holmes' parachute failed to open during a jump, but he survived the fall by landing in a blackberry bush. He holds the record for the highest fall without a parachute.
