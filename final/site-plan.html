Home page

Skydiving Basics: Skydiving is a heart-pounding adventure that starts with a leap from an aircraft thousands of feet above the earth's surface. As you step out into the vast open sky, you'll experience a thrilling freefall before deploying your parachute to slow down and safely land. On our website, we'll provide an in-depth look at the basic principles of skydiving, explaining concepts like terminal velocity, the science of parachutes, and the history of this exhilarating sport. You'll gain a profound understanding of what makes skydiving an unmatched experience and why it's a passion for countless enthusiasts worldwide. Skydiving is all about experiencing the sensation of unbounded freedom, and to fully appreciate it, you'll want to explore the various types of skydiving. Whether you're a first-time jumper or an experienced adrenaline junkie, our website will guide you through the different skydiving options available. Discover tandem skydiving, where you can leap from an aircraft securely attached to a seasoned instructor, or delve into the world of solo jumps, freefalls, and even canopy relative work. Each type offers a unique adventure, and we'll help you choose the one that matches your aspirations and experience level. Skydiving Training: Becoming a certified skydiver involves a structured training process that equips you with the knowledge and skills needed to jump safely and confidently. On our website, we'll outline the various stages of skydiving training. You'll start with ground school, where you'll learn the fundamentals of skydiving, safety protocols, and the equipment. As you progress, we'll explain the different levels of training, from static line jumps to accelerated freefall (AFF) courses. Finally, you'll gain insights into the licenses required to advance through the sport, such as the United States Parachute Association's A-license and beyond. Training is the key to unlocking the thrilling world of skydiving, and our website is dedicated to helping you understand and navigate this process. We'll introduce you to certified skydiving instructors who play a pivotal role in your training, guiding you through the essential skills and procedures required for safe jumps. Whether you're considering your first jump or looking to earn advanced licenses, our training section will be a valuable resource on your journey to becoming a proficient skydiver.

FAQs

FAQs 1. Is skydiving safe? Skydiving is generally safe when conducted by experienced professionals and following safety protocols. The sport has a low fatality rate, with most accidents occurring due to human error or equipment malfunctions. Training, proper equipment, and adherence to safety guidelines are essential for a safe jump. 2. What is the age limit for skydiving? The minimum age for skydiving varies by location but is typically around 18 years old. Some drop zones may allow younger participants with parental consent. There is no maximum age limit, but individuals above a certain age may require a doctor's approval. 3. Do I need any special skills or experience to go skydiving? No prior experience or special skills are required for tandem skydiving, where you jump with a certified instructor. For solo jumps, you'll need to complete a training program, such as Accelerated Freefall (AFF), which teaches you the necessary skills. 4. How much does a skydive cost? The cost of a skydive varies based on factors like location, jump type, and altitude. On average, tandem jumps can range from $200 to $300, while solo training programs may cost more. Be sure to check with your chosen drop zone for specific pricing. 5. How long does a skydive last? Freefall typically lasts about 30 to 60 seconds, depending on the altitude of the jump. After deploying the parachute, the canopy flight can last an additional 5 to 7 minutes, providing a total skydiving experience of around 10 minutes. 6. What should I wear for a skydive? Wear comfortable clothing, and dress appropriately for the weather conditions. Most drop zones provide jumpsuits to wear over your clothing, and you'll need closed-toe shoes, preferably with laces. 7. Can I bring a camera or GoPro on my jump? Many drop zones allow you to bring a camera or GoPro on your jump, but there are strict safety guidelines for securing the equipment. It's essential to discuss this with your instructor and follow their instructions to ensure safe use. 8. What does it feel like to jump out of an airplane? The sensation of jumping out of an airplane is a mix of exhilaration, adrenaline, and freedom. As you exit the aircraft, you'll experience a rush of wind and a brief moment of weightlessness during the freefall. It's an indescribable feeling that's both thrilling and awe-inspiring. 9. What happens if the parachute doesn't open? Modern parachute systems are equipped with redundancies and automatic activation devices (AADs) that deploy the reserve parachute if the main one fails to open. Fatal parachute failures are extremely rare, thanks to these safety features and proper training. 10. Can I eat before a skydive? It's advisable to eat a light, balanced meal a few hours before your jump to maintain energy levels. Avoid heavy or greasy foods, as they may cause discomfort during the jump. Staying hydrated is also essential. However, it's best to follow the specific dietary recommendations provided by your drop zone to ensure a safe and comfortable experience.

Records and acomplishments

records and acomplishments Highest Altitude Jump: Alan Eustace set a record in 2014 by jumping from a helium balloon at an altitude of 135,890 feet, breaking the sound barrier during his descent. Most Jumps in 24 Hours: Jay Stokes completed 640 jumps in a 24-hour period in 2006, setting a world record for the most jumps in a single day. Oldest Skydiver: In 2019, Bryson William Verdun Hayes became the oldest skydiver at the age of 101. He made a tandem jump to celebrate his centenary. Fastest Freefall Speed: Felix Baumgartner broke the speed of sound during his famous Red Bull Stratos jump in 2012, reaching a maximum freefall speed of 843.6 mph (1,357.6 km/h). Longest Freefall Time: In 2016, Luke Aikins completed a jump from 25,000 feet without a parachute and landed safely in a net. He achieved the longest freefall without a parachute. Largest Formation: In 2015, 164 jumpers from 7 different countries set a record for the largest formation skydive, creating a formation in the shape of a giant flower. Wingsuit Records: Multiple records exist for wingsuit flying, including the farthest distance flown, longest time in a wingsuit, and highest altitude jump wearing a wingsuit. Jumping Without a Parachute and Surviving: In 2007, Michael Holmes' parachute failed to open during a jump, but he survived the fall by landing in a blackberry bush. He holds the record for the highest fall without a parachute.
